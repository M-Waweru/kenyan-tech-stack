# The Kenyan Tech Stack

**Rails, Hustle, and the Republic of Builders**

**Mathenge** — [mwaweru.com](https://mwaweru.com) · [mathnuscript.com](https://mathnuscript.com)

Kenya did not become a global technology reference point by building apps first. It built **rails** first: connectivity, distribution, messaging, identity, payments, and APIs. Those rails then reshaped behavior in commerce, education, governance, logistics, credit, media, and labor.

## Why I wanted to write this

I’m **Mathenge**. I wanted a single, honest map of **who and what actually shaped** Kenya’s digital life—not only the founders on conference stages, but the **referees** (regulators, banks, telcos), the **users** who bent products into real habits, and the **infrastructure** that made any of it possible. Too often the story gets flattened into “Kenya is a fintech miracle” without showing the **sequence of rails**, the friction, the failures, or the millions of people who learned USSD, queues, and WhatsApp long before anyone said “platform.” This book is my attempt to tell that thicker story: **rails first**, behavior second, myth last—and to hold space for the shadow side (fraud, exclusion, concentration) as part of the same stack, not an afterthought.

## How this book was made

I did not type every sentence in a vacuum. **I shaped the thesis, the chapter architecture, and the lens**—who counts as a protagonist, what “the stack” means, how builder–user–referee shows up in each era. The drafting and iteration drew heavily on **AI tools**: **ChatGPT**, **Cursor**, and **Codex** (and similar assistants) for prose, structure, research pointers, and cleanup. **I remain responsible for the framing and for pushing toward cited, checkable claims** where the book makes them; the [Source Catalog](appendices/sources.md) and chapter-level links are part of that discipline. Think of it as **human direction plus machine amplification**, not an anonymous auto-book—and if something reads wrong, the fault lies with me and the edit, not with “the model” in the abstract. More from me: [mwaweru.com](https://mwaweru.com), [mathnuscript.com](https://mathnuscript.com).

**This folder (`book/`) is the published site:** topic chapters, prologue, and appendices only. Outlines, merged drafts, audits, and workflow docs for editors live in the repo’s **`authoring/`** directory (not deployed with the book). If you are **writing or expanding chapters**, read **[Authoring — agent chapter guide](../authoring/AGENT-CHAPTER-GUIDE.md)** (or repo root **[AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md)**).

**Topic layout:** Each chapter is a **long-form topic article** under `part-NN/` (one markdown file per company, rail, or theme). Section order and editorial rules are documented under `authoring/meta/` for maintainers.

**Recurring motifs:** agent stall • cybercafé • WhatsApp group • OTP • Till number • PayBill • a queue that became a workflow • a meme that became a movement.
