# 11. What M-PESA Did to the Ecosystem

## Lead

The strongest proof of infrastructure is behavioral spillover. Mobile money's effect appears in sectors that do not market themselves as fintech: school administration, public fee collection, transportation payments, digital fundraising, micro-insurance, supplier management, and on-demand logistics payouts.

The rail's ecosystem role is settlement confidence. Builders in many sectors designed their products around an assumption that payment confirmation could be near-real-time and broadly familiar to users. This reduced onboarding burden and enabled business models that require frequent low-value transactions.

The user experience shifted from occasional digital payment to expected digital payment. That expectation shaped interface design, service recovery protocols, and customer-support norms.

The shadow is systemic dependency. A dominant settlement channel creates single-point stress during outages, policy shocks, or security incidents.

Bridge: outside Kenya, many tried to replicate this model and learned that rails depend on context.
