# 25. Kenya's High-Profile Hacks: Attack Surface of a Digitizing State

## Lead

When a public platform is disrupted, the effects are immediate: delayed filings, blocked payments, anxious users, and reputational shock. Technical incidents become political events because citizens experience them as service breakdown.

The rail under test is national digital trust. Builders include government SOC teams, incident responders, platform engineers, and communication teams managing public updates. Users include businesses and citizens dependent on continuity. Referees include telecom regulator functions, cyber policy bodies, and legal institutions.

Behavior shifted toward stronger demand for resilience, backup processes, and transparent incident communication. Public understanding of cybersecurity improved, but so did skepticism.

The shadow is compounding distrust if disruptions recur without visible accountability and remediation.

Bridge: sustaining a digital state requires talent pipelines, literacy, and operational culture that begin in education systems.
