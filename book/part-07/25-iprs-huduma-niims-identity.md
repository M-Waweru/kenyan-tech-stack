# 25. IPRS, Huduma, NIIMS: Identity Wars

## Lead

A person standing at a service point hears, "Your record is missing" or "You need another verification step." In that moment, identity is no longer abstract policy. It is access or denial.

The rail is national identity infrastructure linked to service delivery. Builders include registry system architects, administrators, and policy teams. Users are citizens whose ability to transact depends on data integrity and recognition. Referees include courts, rights groups, data-protection regulators, and parliament.

Behavior changed because identity became prerequisite for participation across multiple rails: finance, education, public services, and compliance workflows. The convenience upside is large when records are accurate and interoperable.

The shadow includes privacy concerns, exclusion risk, and governance legitimacy challenges. Identity systems can magnify both efficiency and inequity.

Bridge: as identity and service rails centralized, they became attractive targets for cyber disruption.
