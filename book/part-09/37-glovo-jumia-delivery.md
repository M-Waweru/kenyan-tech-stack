# 37. Glovo, Jumia, and Delivery Unit Economics

## Lead

On the **customer** screen, delivery is a **progress** bar and a rider photo. In the **operations** room, it is **unit economics**: **gross merchandise value (GMV)**, **courier** **utilisation**, **batching**, rain-season **slippage**, and **cash**-on-delivery leakage. **Jumia**’s **2019** **New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)** listing story—**TechCrunch** citing **13 million+** packages in **2018**, **Jumia Food** as a vertical, and **trucks** and **motorbikes** “ubiquitous” with **Lagos** street life—makes the physical rail explicit: pan-African **e-commerce** had to grow its own logistics muscle, not inherit **Amazon**-grade post ([TechCrunch — Jumia NYSE IPO][tc-jumia]).

**Glovo**—the **Barcelona**-born multi-category **courier** app—shows the other face: **Delivery Hero**’s **July 2022** close of a majority stake described **Glovo** operating in **25** countries across **Europe**, **Central Asia**, and **Africa**, while the combined group narrative reached **74** countries and up to **2.2 billion** people served ([Delivery Hero — Glovo close][dh-glovo]). **Kenya** sits inside that footprint as one of many **urban** arenas where **last-mile** promises meet **traffic**, **security**, and wage politics.

The **rail** is on-demand **last-mile**—restaurant bags, **dark-store** groceries, pharmacy runs—stacked on the same **smartphone**/**data** adoption curves that enabled [35. Uber and Bolt](35-uber-bolt-driver-backlash.md). **Builders** are **marketplace** operators, **merchant** onboarding teams, **routing** engineers, and **rider** **fleet** managers. **Users** are office workers ordering lunch, **small and medium enterprise (SME)** restaurants, and **gig** couriers trading **bicycle** wear for daily **float**. **Referees** are **municipal** **traffic** rules, **labour** discourse, **investor** **burn** tolerance, and—when platforms hold **location** trails—**Kenya**’s **Data Protection Act**, **2019** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]).

**Uber**’s **Africa** leadership told **TechCrunch** in **2016** that **Nigeria**’s **Jumia** and **Konga** “created their own delivery fleets” where professional logistics thinly existed—an indirect acknowledgment that **person**-moving networks and **parcel** networks blur when **utilisation** is king ([TechCrunch — Uber Africa, logistics aside][tc-uber-logistics]).

The tension is **speed versus sustainability**. Subsidised **ETAs** train **customers**; **fuel** prices and **courier** pay **untrain** them.

[Part 10](../part-10/index.md) picks up **business-to-business (B2B)** **supply** rails where consumer apps leave off.

## History

### Jumia: build the sled first

**Jumia**’s **IPO** coverage highlighted **Rocket Internet** roots, **14** markets, **JumiaPay**, **seller** **marketplaces**, and years of losses typical of **infrastructure-heavy** **e-commerce**—including **freight** as a dominant **fulfilment** cost line in **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)** filings referenced by **TechCrunch** ([TechCrunch — Jumia NYSE IPO][tc-jumia]).

### Glovo inside Delivery Hero

The **Glovo** **close** press release emphasised brand continuity, ongoing **management** led by co-founders **Oscar Pierre** and **Sacha Michaud**, and **geographic** complementarity—**mergers and acquisitions (M&A)** as **cap table** engineering, not necessarily **user experience (UX)** simplification for riders ([Delivery Hero — Glovo close][dh-glovo]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: Two orders, one rider

*Composite, grounded in batching economics common to multi-category apps:* A **courier** collects a **pharmacy** pouch and a **burger** bag in **Westlands**, then watches the **app** **re-sequence** drops when the second customer does not answer—**time** **penalties** eat **hourly** yield.

Mechanically, **on-demand** stacks need **merchant** **point-of-sale** **sync**, **hygiene** **service-level agreements (SLAs)**, **cash** **float** management, and **fraud** controls on **promo** abuse.

## Business model and incentives

**Marketplaces** tax **GMV** through commissions and **delivery** fees; **couriers** are paid per drop or per hour with **bonus** ladders that mirror ride-hail **quest** design. **Jumia**’s public **financial** narrative tied hope to **mix** shift toward **services** and **payments**—anything to dilute pure **freight** drag ([TechCrunch — Jumia NYSE IPO][tc-jumia]).

## Regulation and referees

**Labour** classification debates follow riders across continents. **Personal** data—**GPS** traces, phone numbers—requires lawful basis and retention discipline under the **Data Protection Act**, **2019** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]) and **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)** expectations ([Office of the Data Protection Commissioner][odpc]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**Urban** **Nairobi**-biased adoption mirrors ride-hail: density, **restaurant** supply, and **middle-class** **time** value converge first; peri-urban corridors follow as **courier** supply deepens.

## Ecosystem effects

**Delivery** apps trained merchants to think in **tablet** **tickets** and **rating** hygiene; they also pressured **landlords** and **malls** to carve **rider** parking inches.

## Setbacks and controversies

**Consolidation**, **layoffs**, and **route** **deactivation** spikes are structural when **venture** **subsidies** pause. **Jumia**’s **IPO** era included **authenticity** and governance debates **TechCrunch** documented—useful reminders that “**Africa** **tech**” narratives are fought, not given ([TechCrunch — Jumia NYSE IPO][tc-jumia]).

## Competition and alternatives

Phone-a-kitchen **runners**, **boda** **stage** favours, **WhatsApp** group orders, and **supermarket** **own-fleet** **delivery** compete with app **marketplaces**.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: **last-mile** became a first-class **Kenyan** **consumer** **category**, not an **e-commerce** afterthought. Open questions: **quick-commerce** **dark stores**, **aggregator** **regulation**, and **electric vehicle (EV)** **bikes** at **grid** **tariff** realities.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.* Model **rain**, **security** stops, and **cash** refunds—**Silicon Valley** **ETA** curves rarely survive **El Niño** afternoons. **Publish** **courier** **pay** **transparency** if you want **political** quiet.
