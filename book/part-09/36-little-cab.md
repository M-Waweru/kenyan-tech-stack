# 36. Little Cab: The Local Counterpunch

## Lead

**July 2016**: **Safaricom**—already **Kenya**’s dominant **telecom** and **M-PESA** rail—enters ride-hail through **Little Cab**, built with **Nairobi** software house **Craft Silicon**. **TechCrunch** summarises the bundle: **iOS**, **Android**, **Windows**; **cash**, card, and **M-PESA**; live **GPS** maps; free **Safaricom** **Wi-Fi** for passengers; and soon **unstructured supplementary service data (USSD)** access for non-**smartphone** users ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]). The release positioning is aggressive on economics: **KSh 55** per kilometre pitched against **Uber**’s **KSh 60**, and “no more than **15%**” taken from drivers versus “**Uber**’s **25%**” in the same story’s framing ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

The **rail** is **local** **integration** **density**—telecom brand trust, **mobile money** checkout, distribution psychology—applied to the same **two-sided** **dispatch** physics as [35. Uber and Bolt](35-uber-bolt-driver-backlash.md). **Builders** are **Safaricom** product and business teams (the piece quotes business director **Rita Okuthe** on **Wi-Fi** as a cross-sell surface) and **Craft Silicon** under **Kamal Budhabhatti**, who tells **TechCrunch** **Little** aims for “**one million** rides in the next six months” while “entrenching” a homegrown story ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]). **Users** are riders and drivers optimising net pay and network quality. **Referees** are the same **NTSA**/**MoU** politics later surfaced in **2019** strikes—**Little** appears alongside **Uber** and **Bolt** in that reporting ([The Star — Uber Bolt strike, MoU][star-mou])—plus everyday **competition** on fares.

**Reuters**’ **August 2016** strike reporting already named **Little Cab** as the joint **Safaricom**/**Craft Silicon** venture launched **July 5**, and quoted drivers threatening wholesale defection from **Uber** ([Reuters — Kenya Uber strike][reuters-uber]).

The tension is **home-field advantage versus global capital**. **Safaricom** can bundle; **Uber** can subsidise narratives and iterate pricing overnight—**TechCrunch** confirmed **Uber**’s **35%** fare cut the same week **Little**’s pricing story broke ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

## Context

**Little** assumes the stack from [Part II](../part-02/index.md) (**subscriber identity module (SIM)** penetration, **data**, **M-PESA**) and rides the same urban **mobility** hunger **Uber** stoked in **2015** ([35. Uber and Bolt](35-uber-bolt-driver-backlash.md)).

## History

### Partnership mechanics

**Craft Silicon** supplied software credibility; **Safaricom** supplied brand, **Wi-Fi**, and **payment** rails—classic **Kenyan** pattern: **local** **integrator** meets **platform** **scale** ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

### Competitive spiral

**Bitange Ndemo**, quoted in **TechCrunch**, predicted bundled vehicle services and **Wi-Fi** arms races—accurate as rhetorical direction even where implementation varied by fleet ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: M-PESA settles before the door opens

*Composite, grounded in **TechCrunch** payment and **Wi-Fi** details:* A rider completes a **cash** trip but the next leg is **M-PESA** from the back seat while the dongle blinks; **Little**’s **Wi-Fi** splash page advertises **Safaricom** retail SKUs—mobility as **distribution** for **telecom** SKUs ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

Mechanically, **Little**-class apps must still solve driver onboarding, **vehicle** financing partnerships, fraud, and support **tickets** at **Safaricom** scale expectations.

## Business model and incentives

Lower stated take rates and **M-PESA** integration shift margin to drivers *if* sustained; **cross-sell** through **Wi-Fi** and subscriber lock-in is the **strategic** prize **Okuthe** names explicitly ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

## Regulation and referees

**Little** is not outside labour politics—**2019** **MoU** reporting lists it beside **Uber** and **Bolt** as a signatory party drivers blamed for stalled implementation ([The Star — Uber Bolt strike, MoU][star-mou]). Trip and payment data remain sensitive under the **Data Protection Act**, **2019** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**TechCrunch** relayed **Uber**’s contemporaneous **Kenya** claims—**one million** trips and **100,000** monthly app opens in **Nairobi**—as competitive backdrop; verify before treating as enduring metrics ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

## Ecosystem effects

**Little** proved incumbents could ship credible **hail** products without waiting for **Silicon Valley** blessing—signal **MTN**/**Airtel**-class operators across the continent noticed ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]).

## Setbacks and controversies

Commission and fare figures in launch-era press were **battle** **marketing**, not audited **financials**. Driver **multi-homing** means loyalty is never guaranteed ([TechCrunch — Little Cab vs Uber][tc-little]; [Reuters — Kenya Uber strike][reuters-uber]).

## Competition and alternatives

[35. Uber and Bolt](35-uber-bolt-driver-backlash.md), legacy taxis, and **matatu** networks remain substitutes for many corridors.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: **Kenya** showed **telecom** × **software** joint ventures can enter **mobility** with payment-native UX. Open questions: **EV** financing, **super-app** bundling, and whether **regulators** will cap take rates.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.* If you partner a **telco**, clarify who owns **customer** **support** **service-level agreements (SLAs)** and fraud **escalation** on day one. **Wi-Fi** and **M-PESA** are features; **driver** **net** pay is the retention loop.
