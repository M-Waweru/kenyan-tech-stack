# Part 5: Banks Become Platforms (2014–today)

Part V is where regulated banking meets developer surfaces and app-first retail: **Equity**-backed **Finserve** and **Jenga** (**banking-as-a-service (BaaS)** plumbing), neobank-shaped brands (**LOOP**, **Fingo**, **Branch**, **Umba**), and incumbent retail apps that became table stakes ([chapter 18](18-retail-banking-app-table-stakes.md)). The interpretive question is not which logo wins—it is what behaviour became normal after bank capability turned into APIs, partnerships, downloads, and permanent mobile roadmaps.

- [17. Neobank Wave: Banking as UX](17-neobank-wave-banking-ux.md)

## How to read this part

- Chapter 16 is the integrator story: banking-as-a-service primitives, Finserve/Jenga, CBK and data-protection referees, dependency risk.
- Chapter 17 is the neobank and cross-border digital bank story: LOOP, Fingo, Branch, Umba, profitability pressure, BaaS/open finance as plumbing.
- Chapter 18 is the incumbent mirror image: KCB, Equity, Absa, Co-op, I&M, StanChart—why every tier-one bank now ships and revamps apps as default strategy.

Conflict to watch: velocity versus compliance—the same APIs and onboarding flows that include people also invite fraud and regulatory backlash.
