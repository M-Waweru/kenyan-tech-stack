# 16. Jenga API and the Platformization of Banking

## Lead

A fintech founder who once spent months negotiating manual bank integrations now calls an endpoint and receives structured response data in seconds. That transition is not cosmetic. It changes what kinds of companies can be built and how fast they can reach market.

The rail is banking-as-a-service and API-mediated financial capabilities. Builders include bank digital subsidiaries, integration teams, compliance architects, and developer-relations units. Users include startups, enterprises, and institutions embedding payment and account workflows into their own products. Referees include central bank oversight, AML/KYC obligations, and consumer-risk controls.

Behavior changed as banking moved from venue (branch) to capability (service layer). Businesses began designing around reusable primitives: account verification, transfers, disbursements, reconciliation, and audit trails. Integration literacy became a strategic skill across sectors.

The shadow surfaced in operational dependencies. Outages, schema changes, and compliance constraints could propagate quickly through dependent services. Customer ownership tensions emerged between platform providers and app-layer innovators.

Bridge: once banking functions became programmable, competition shifted toward user experience.
