# 20. DPO: Cross-Border Card Rails

## Lead

The **Safaricom** customer completes a **Lipa na M-PESA** buy in seconds; the **Dubai** tourist’s card authorization for the same hotel chain still has to cross **foreign exchange (FX)**, **fraud** scoring, and **scheme** rules before anyone sleeps. For airlines, hotels, and **e-commerce** exporters, **card** reliability is not a channel preference—it is revenue physics.

**Eran Feinstein** and **Offer Gat** built **DPO Group** into one of Africa’s largest **payment service provider** footprints—corporate materials and leadership posts frame **Feinstein** as a co-founder **chief executive officer (CEO)** who steered the company through scale and into **Network International**’s acquisition close, after which he handed regional leadership forward ([DPO — Feinstein transition post][dpo-eran]). The **rail** is **card**-centric **gateway** infrastructure with **risk** tooling—hosted checkout, **application programming interface (API)** integrations, multi-currency acceptance, and **mobile money** adjacency where markets demand it (**DPO** lists **M-PESA** among methods on its corporate surfaces) ([DPO Pay corporate site][dpo-site]). **Builders** are **fraud** analysts, routing engineers, and **merchant** success teams who live inside **chargeback** ratios. **Users** are travel desks, **SaaS** billers, and **SME** shops that must clear **Visa**/**Mastercard** without owning **Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)** compliance end to end. **Referees** are **scheme** rules, **CBK** and peer **central bank** policy, and dispute regimes that can claw back settled optimism.

The tension is **conversion versus control**: every extra **fraud** check protects **chargeback** exposure but risks basket abandonment. The **shadow** is **dispute** drag—**card** rails are fast until they are lawyer-slow.

## Context

**M-PESA** trained **Kenya** on instant retail receipts; **card** rails still dominate cross-border **business-to-consumer** flows and many high-ticket locals. **DPO**’s story intersects **Nairobi**’s hub habit—regional **e-commerce** and travel distribution often centralise technical integration in **Kenya** while clearing **Africa**-wide traffic ([*Kenya’s Payments Journey*][cbk-payments] for broader payments modernisation backdrop). [Chapter 19](19-cellulant.md) named orchestration across wallets and banks; **DPO** names the **card** backbone that stayed stubbornly global.

## History

### Pilot instincts to PSP scale

Corporate narrative ties **DPO**’s origins to solving **online** **booking** acceptance gaps for **African** merchants—founders pitching reliability where **international** **card** traffic met local operational reality ([DPO — Feinstein transition post][dpo-eran]). The public arc culminated in **October 2021**, when **Network International** completed acquisition of **DPO Group** for **USD 291.3 million**—**ITWeb Africa**’s **Chris Tredger** reported the close from **Johannesburg** with the same headline numbers and executive quotes, giving readers a bylined story alongside the parties’ own releases ([ITWeb Africa — Network × DPO][itweb-dpo]) ([DPO — acquisition closes][dpo-close], [Network International press][ni-dpo]).

### Under new corporate gravity

Post-acquisition branding converged on **DPO Pay by Network** language on corporate pages—same rails, different **balance sheet** and **compliance** superstructure ([DPO — acquisition closes][dpo-close]). For **Kenyan** **builders**, the lesson is familiar: gateways **exit** into **payments** platforms; **merchant** contracts survive as assignments and integrations keep working—until they do not.

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: the travel desk at 10 p.m.

*Composite:* A **Nairobi** agency issues tickets for a **European** tour group. **Cards** authorise in euros; settlement lands in shillings after **FX** spread. **Risk** flags a **velocity** pattern; **support** wakes a **duty** manager. Nobody tweets about the **application programming interface**—only about whether seats confirm.

Mechanically, **PSPs** provide **tokenisation**-adjacent patterns, **3-D Secure** flows where required, **webhook** callbacks, reconciliation dashboards, and **mobile money** rails as add-ons for local completion ([DPO Pay corporate site][dpo-site]).

## Business model and incentives

**Interchange**-driven **economics** sit upstream; **PSP** revenue mixes **transaction** fees, **FX** markup, and **value-added** services (**fraud** tools, **installment** partnerships). Incentives push **volume** and **active** **merchant** growth—**Network International**’s **investor** communications after close highlighted **DPO**’s growth contribution, signalling how **Africa** **card** throughput entered listed-company storytelling ([Network International press][ni-dpo]).

## Regulation and referees

### Referee beat — scheme compliance as silent law

**Card** networks set **PCI** expectations, **chargeback** evidence standards, and **high-risk** **merchant** category rules that reshape onboarding overnight. **CBK**-era **payments** modernisation and **consumer** protection culture influence how **disputes** and **refunds** must behave in **Kenya** even when the **scheme** is global ([*Kenya’s Payments Journey*][cbk-payments]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**Kenya** hosts regional **commercial** and **technical** talent for **DPO**-class firms; adoption shows up wherever **SME**s sell to **diaspora** **cardholders** or **businesses** invoice in **foreign currency**. **M-PESA**-first **checkout** still wins many domestic carts; **card** wins **international** intent.

## Ecosystem effects

**DPO**-era infrastructure **lowered the shame** of “we cannot take **Visa** online” for mid-market **African** merchants. It also **prepared** institutional buyers to expect **digital** **receipts**—the same expectation **JamboPay** and county **e-collections** would exploit in adjacent chapters ([chapter 21](21-jambopay.md)).

## Setbacks and controversies

**Chargebacks**, **rolling** **reserves**, and **fraud** busts hit **merchants** harder than **consumers** see. **Acquisition** integrations occasionally strand **legacy** **merchant** IDs or **pricing** tiers. **FX** volatility becomes a political topic when **statements** surprise.

## Competition and alternatives

Global **gateways**, **bank** **merchant** **acquirers**, **M-PESA** **Daraja** flows, and **neo**-**PSPs** compete on fee, **latency**, and **support**. **Multi-rail** fluency—**card** plus **mobile money**—is table stakes for serious **checkout** teams ([chapter 19](19-cellulant.md), [chapter 22](22-pesapal.md)).

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: **African** **card** acceptance became a **product** category with **Kenyan** **integration** gravity, not only **European** **payment** **facilitators** selling into **Lagos** from afar. Open questions: **local** **scheme** alternatives, **real-time** **push** payments versus **card** **pull**, and how **listed** **parents** prioritise **Africa** **margin** versus **growth**.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.*

Assume **chargebacks** are **product** failures until proven otherwise—**evidence** packets, **descriptor** strings, and **customer** **support** **SLAs** matter as much as **code**. **Multi-currency** **pricing** without **hedging** **discipline** is a **balance** **sheet** **toy**.
