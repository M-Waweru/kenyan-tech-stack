## Fact Classes and Confidence

For future editorial hardening, classify claims per chapter as:

- Class A: legal texts, regulator reports, official statements.
- Class B: major financial reporting and primary company disclosures.
- Class C: interviews, field reporting, and observed workflows.
- Class D: analytical interpretation and narrative framing.

Every chapter should include at least one Class A or B anchor before publication.

## Source Prioritization Rules

1. Prefer Kenyan official institutions first for laws, regulations, and service data.
2. Use company annual reports and official product announcements for roadmap claims.
3. Use reputable international reporting for market events requiring independent verification.
4. Use interviews to explain behavior and incentives, not to replace factual anchors.

## Safety and Ethics Protocol

- Do not publish operational detail that could enable fraud or exploitation.
- Anonymize vulnerable interviewees by default where risk exists.
- Separate allegation from confirmed fact explicitly.
- Use trauma-informed interview practice for high-risk labor and abuse contexts.
- Legal review required for chapters involving active disputes or named allegations.

## Publication-Ready Upgrade Checklist

1. Add citation footnotes in each chapter linked to exact source URLs.
2. Add timeline boxes with exact dates and legal references.
3. Add data exhibits (adoption, incidents, growth curves) per part.
4. Add interview attribution table (on-record, background, anonymous).
5. Run external fact-check and legal sensitivity review.
6. Final copy edit for tone and consistency.
