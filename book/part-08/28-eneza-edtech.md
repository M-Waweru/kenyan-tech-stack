# 28. Eneza: Feature-Phone EdTech

## Lead

After dishes and homework copies, a learner thumbs **short message service (SMS)** prompts in the dark: multiple choice, a wrong-answer explanation, another question tomorrow. No **broadband** romance—just **airtime** arithmetic and the hope that **10** **Kenyan shillings (KSh)** a week buys enough reps to climb the class rank. That is **Eneza Education**’s original bet: meet **Kenya**’s **exam machine** ([27. Kenya's Exam Machine](27-kenya-exam-machine.md)) on the handset most households already own.

The **rail** is **low-bandwidth** **curriculum**-aligned practice—**SMS** first, later **Android** and web layers—pitched as a “virtual tutor” and teacher assistant. **Builders** are co-founders **Toni Maraviglia** (teacher-trained product instinct) and **Kago Kagichiri** (engineering), plus content teams who map lessons to national syllabi. **Users** are upper-primary and secondary students, **drop-outs**, and parents buying peace of mind in **shillings** per week. **Referees** are **school** acceptance of third-party study tools, **Safaricom**-era **telecom** economics, and honest alignment with **KNEC**-shaped classroom reality.

**BBC News** profiled the company in **2015**: **~500,000** users in **Kenya**, **~70,000** monthly active, **KSh 10** weekly deducted from **prepaid** airtime, courses in maths, science, **Kiswahili**, and English framed as aligned to the national curriculum, plus gamified hooks like a detective story and points ([BBC News — Changing Kenya's education by phone][bbc-eneza]). Treat those figures as **period** journalism, not live dashboards—but the **insight** is durable: **for-profit** **mission** pricing at **margins** that only work at **continent** scale.

The tension is **reach versus depth**. **SMS** can’t carry rich labs; it can carry **drills** that beat silence.

## Context

**Eneza** landed when **prepaid** **SIM** penetration already made **mobile** the national **computer** ([Part I](../part-01/index.md), [Part III](../part-03/index.md)). **Feature-phone** **USSD** and **SMS** literacy from **M-PESA** menus lowered the UI barrier. **Exam** pressure supplied willingness to pay recurring micro-fees ([27. Kenya's Exam Machine](27-kenya-exam-machine.md)).

## History

### 2011 Nairobi: teacher meets technologist

Maraviglia’s quoted motivation—classrooms of **70–80** pupils and impossible daily **feedback** loops—frames the product as **labour** arbitrage: outsource spaced repetition to the **network** ([BBC News — Changing Kenya's education by phone][bbc-eneza]). **Kagichiri**’s role in the same piece is classic **Kenyan** stack DNA: the technologist who “hated teachers” meets the teacher who “hated technology,” then ships something parents quote on record ([BBC News — Changing Kenya's education by phone][bbc-eneza]).

### Beyond Kenya

The **BBC** noted expansion experiments in **Tanzania** and **Ghana**—the predictable **East African** copy path once **playbooks** stabilise ([BBC News — Changing Kenya's education by phone][bbc-eneza]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: Dadaab revision

*Composite, grounded in the **BBC**’s reference to learners in **Dadaab**:* A student without laptop or stable **Wi-Fi** still completes nightly **SMS** quizzes. Teachers receive performance summaries where **school** infrastructure allows partnership. The **product** is **asynchronous** tutoring—closer to **spaced repetition** than to **MOOC** theatre.

Mechanically, **Eneza**-class systems need **short code** partnerships, **billing** integrations, **content** versioning per syllabus change, and **fraud** resistance on answer keys circulating offline.

## Business model and incentives

**Airtime**-deducted **microsubscriptions** align revenue with **mass** usage but expose the business to **telecom** policy and **VAT** noise. **B2B** school licences and donor pilots historically soften consumer churn; verify current lines on **Eneza**’s own site before treating **BBC** **2015** pricing as today’s truth.

## Regulation and referees

**CA** governs **telecom** **messaging** rails; **consumer** protection applies to **billing** surprises. **Data Protection Act** governs identifiable **learner** analytics once dashboards move to cloud ([Data Protection Act][dpa]). **Curriculum** claims invite **KICD**-adjacent scrutiny when marketing overreaches ([31. KICD and Curriculum Governance](31-kicd-curriculum-governance.md)).

## Adoption in Kenya

Adoption skews to students with handset access and parental **airtime** subsidy—still broader than laptop edtech. **Rural** **reach** is the moral centre of the **SMS** thesis ([BBC News — Changing Kenya's education by phone][bbc-eneza]).

## Ecosystem effects

**Eneza** proved **examinable** **drills** could ride **GSM** signalling, not **fibre**. It set expectations for [29. BRCK](29-brck-offline-classroom.md)-style **offline** **classroom** kits and for [32. School Ops](32-school-ops-fees-messaging.md) products that still default to **SMS** and **WhatsApp** for parent comms.

## Setbacks and controversies

**Content** **staleness** when **syllabi** pivot (**CBC**), **churn** when **airtime** runs dry, and **equity** critiques (“**digital** **homework** widens gaps”) recur for all **mobile** edtech.

## Competition and alternatives

**YouTube** **cram** channels, **Facebook** groups, **private** **tuition** centres, and global apps with **zero** marginal cost compete for attention. **Eneza**’s moat was **pricing** and **channel** fit, not **graphics**.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: **Kenyan** edtech learned to price in **shillings** per week, not dollars per month. Open questions: **smartphone** saturation vs **SMS** **ARPU**, **AI** tutors on **WhatsApp**, and **regulatory** treatment of **automated** **messaging** to minors.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.*

If you **SMS**-teach, own **delivery** **latency** and **failure** **SMS** copy. **Cache** **content** on-device where possible. **Publish** **curriculum** mapping and revision dates—schools **fire** vendors who miss **syllabus** shifts.
