# 38. Twiga: Produce as a Machine

## Lead

Before dawn, informal retailers still haggle in wet markets—but **Peter Njonjo**’s pitch to **Reuters** in **2019** was that **Africa**’s informal food system could be *addressable* if demand were aggregated on a screen: **Twiga Foods** estimated that system at **USD 300 billion** a year and growing, and described an online link between growers and shops where a company **fleet** could move fruit and vegetables to retailers within **18 hours** of an order while paying farmers within **48 hours** over **mobile** rails such as **M-PESA** ([Reuters — Twiga raises USD 30M][reuters-twiga]).

The **rail** is **business-to-business (B2B)** produce and **fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG)** distribution with **working-capital** velocity—cold rooms, conveyors, and sorting infrastructure at a new **Nairobi** distribution centre named in the same wire as the growth plan ([Reuters — Twiga raises USD 30M][reuters-twiga]). **Builders** are co-founders **Njonjo** and **Grant Brooke**, field buyers, **logistics** planners, and later marketplace teams when the model thins asset-heavy legs. **Users** are farmers needing faster cash cycles and kiosk owners needing predictable stock. **Referees** are **venture** **liquidity**, **fuel** and **forex** shocks, and the blunt arithmetic of perishability.

**August 2023** brought a different referee beat: **TechCrunch** reported **Twiga** laying off **283** people—**33%** of an **850**-person workforce—ending in-house delivery supported by leased trucks, shuttering **10** **Nairobi** distribution centres, and opening a **logistics** **marketplace** for independent truckers while consolidating into a **200,000**-square-foot **Tatu City** warehouse. **Njonjo** cited macro headwinds, tighter **venture** capital, and customers’ declining purchasing power ([TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-restructure]).

The tension is **throughput romance versus working capital**. Every hour of shelf life saved is an hour of **diesel**, **debt**, and **payroll** financed.

## Context

**Twiga** sits downstream of [Part III](../part-03/index.md) (**M-PESA** as settlement rail) and [Part II](../part-02/index.md) (**mobile** penetration that lets kiosk owners place orders without a desktop). It competes for mindshare with [37. Glovo, Jumia, and Delivery](../part-09/37-glovo-jumia-delivery.md)-class **last-mile**—but optimises for *stall* economics, not *couch* economics.

## History

### 2014–2019: institutional money meets informal retail

**Reuters** framed **Twiga** as part of a wave of **African** startups using **information technology** to crack fragmented informal retail—**Goldman Sachs** leading **USD 30 million** in equity and debt, with **International Finance Corporation (IFC)**, **TLcom Capital**, and **Creadev** among existing investors; cumulative funding reached **USD 55 million** after the round ([Reuters — Twiga raises USD 30M][reuters-twiga]).

### 2023: asset-light pivot under pressure

The **TechCrunch** restructure piece documents the operational U-turn: contractor haulage instead of owned delivery, commission-based sales agents replacing an earlier in-house sales dismissal wave, and explicit commentary on **cost of capital** for **venture**-backed operators ([TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-restructure]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: Tomato clock

*Composite, grounded in **Reuters** time-boxing:* A **mama mboga**’s **SMS**-confirmed order enters **Twiga**’s **dashboard**; **picking** and **routing** must beat the tomato’s tolerance curve; **M-PESA** settles the grower before the retailer’s cash day ends—when the machine works, the product is *time compression*.

Mechanically, **B2B** food platforms must own **quality** grading, **reverse** **logistics** on rejections, **temperature** compliance, and **fraud** on phantom deliveries.

## Business model and incentives

Revenue mixes **margin** on produce, **service** fees on **marketplace** legs, and **data** products sold to **brands**—each line competes for the same operations budget when **fuel** spikes.

## Regulation and referees

Food safety, **county** market fees, and **competition** scrutiny sit alongside **financial** **conduct** when **credit** products touch kiosks. **Personal** data on orders and payments falls under the **Data Protection Act**, **2019** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**Nairobi**-centric density came first; **Western Kenya** and cross-border **Uganda** depots appear in **Njonjo**’s **2023** comments as operational focus areas—some buyers reportedly faced ordering friction during optimisation ([TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-restructure]).

## Ecosystem effects

**Twiga** helped normalise “informal retail as a **data** **segment**” for **FMCG** multinationals and **DFI** investors—language later echoed by [41. MarketForce and Copia](41-marketforce-copia.md).

## Setbacks and controversies

Layoffs, supplier **payment** delays reported in trade press, and **CEO**/**board** transitions after **2023** belong in any honest epilogue—pair **TechCrunch**’s operational reporting with local journalism for personnel moves ([TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-restructure]).

## Competition and alternatives

Traditional **brokers**, **market** **cartels**, **Sokoni** **WhatsApp** chains, and smaller **B2B** apps compete lane by lane.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: proof that **Kenyan** **kiosk** demand could be **tokenised** into **working-capital** instruments. Open questions: **contract** farming **quality**, **climate** volatility, and whether **asset-light** **marketplaces** can still guarantee *freshness* **service-level agreements (SLAs)**.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.* **Perishables** punish **growth** **hacks**. Model **diesel** and **interest** rates before **customer** acquisition cost. When **venture** tightens, your **warehouse** **lease** does not.
