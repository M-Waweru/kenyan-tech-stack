# 39. Lori: Trucking as Software and Negotiation

## Lead

Cross-border freight depends on many variables outside software control: border procedures, customs timing, route disruptions, and payment assurance. Digital tools add visibility, but negotiations remain central.

The rail is freight matching and tracking infrastructure. Builders include logistics marketplace teams, route optimization analysts, and partner networks. Users include shippers, carriers, and drivers navigating uncertainty. Referees include customs systems, regional policy, and payment guarantees.

Behavior changed through better shipment visibility and utilization efficiency for some corridors.

The shadow is persistent volatility in cross-border operations and working-capital constraints.

Bridge: informal retail platforms brought digital supply-chain logic into kiosk-level commerce.
