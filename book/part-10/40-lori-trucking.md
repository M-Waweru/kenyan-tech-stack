# 40. Lori: Trucking as Software and Negotiation

## Lead

**Josh Sandler**, **chief executive officer (CEO)** and co-founder of **Lori Systems**, told **TechCrunch** in **2018** that he studied **Kenya**’s cost stack from first principles—“when you buy a mango on a farm, it’s half a penny and then in the supermarket it’s **80** cents”—and concluded haulage rigidity and opaque pricing were structural leaks ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]). The same piece positions **Lori** as an **Uber**-like **marketplace** for **trucking**: digitised matching, pricing visibility, and **real-time** tracking across a network that had grown to roughly **2,500** trucks and **70** cargo operators by **September 2018**, with staff rising from **20** to **70** ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

The **referee** beat in that article is explicitly **state**: a partnership with the **Kenyan** government on **last-mile** haulage from **rail**—using **software** to choreograph handoffs where **infrastructure** alone rarely solves the final **kilometre** ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

The **rail** is **long-haul** and **bulk** **freight** coordination—**grain**, **fertiliser**, later “all freight bulk” in **Sandler**’s quote—where **border** delay and **fuel** theft matter as much as **application programming interface (API)** uptime. **Builders** are marketplace engineers, **fleet** **relationship** managers, and **data** scientists modelling **utilisation**. **Users** are shippers, **transporters**, and drivers. **Referees** are **customs**, **weighbridges**, **insurance** underwriters, and **development** **finance** when public **rail** **intermodal** deals are on the table.

**Sandler** also claimed a **17.3%** cost reduction moving bulk grain to **Uganda** through **Lori**’s system—treat as **issuer**-channelled **efficacy**, not an independent audit ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

The tension is **visibility versus trust**. **Software** can show a truck icon moving; **cash-in-transit** **risk** still lives in the cab.

## Context

**Lori** emerged as [Part II](../part-02/index.md) **smartphone** penetration met **East African** trade growth, and as [39. Sendy](39-sendy.md)-style operators proved **last-mile** could raise capital—and flame out without **density**.

## History

### Battlefield validation

**TechCrunch** ties **Lori** to its **Startup Battlefield Africa** win in **Nairobi** (**2017**), noting how visibility on stage translated into hiring leverage despite messy post-win press criticism **Sandler** described as personal ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

### Regional mesh

By **2018** the company operated across **Kenya**, **Uganda**, **Rwanda**, and **South Africa**, with senior hires from **Maersk**-grade **logistics** backgrounds—signal of ambition to bridge **startup** speed with **incumbent** discipline ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: Weighbridge WhatsApp dies here

*Composite, grounded in **border** friction common to **East African** haulage:* A **driver** waits at **Busia** while a **clearing** agent’s **WhatsApp** forwards yet another “small facilitation.” **Lori**’s app shows **geofence** arrival; it does not automatically dissolve **institutional** **delay**—that remains negotiation.

Mechanically, freight **marketplaces** need **document** **packs**, **escrow** or **factoring** partners, **fuel** advance controls, and **dispute** **arbitration** when **tonnage** disagrees arise.

## Business model and incentives

Take rates on **gross** **transaction** value, **subscription** **telematics**, and **financing** **spreads** compete as revenue lines—each implies different **balance-sheet** risk.

## Regulation and referees

**Customs** **unions** (**East African Community (EAC)** context), **axle-load** rules, and **cross-border** **insurance** mandates shape what “optimisation” can mean. **Personal** data on drivers and cargo owners falls under **Kenya**’s **Data Protection Act**, **2019** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**Nairobi** as hub, **Mombasa**/**Malaba** corridors, and **rail** **intermodal** pilots form the **Kenya**-centric adoption map implied by the **2018** government collaboration story ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

## Ecosystem effects

**Lori** helped legitimise **freight** as a **venture** category distinct from **passenger** ride-hail—different **unit economics**, same **smartphone** **orchestration** grammar.

## Setbacks and controversies

Public debates on **representation** and **privilege** in **African** **tech** surfaced around **Battlefield** winners; **Sandler**’s quoted response is part of the primary record readers can weigh ([TechCrunch — Lori and Kenya rail last mile][tc-lori]).

## Competition and alternatives

Phone-based **brokers**, **clearing** **agents**, **Sendy**-class **SME** **trucks**, and multinational **forwarders**.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: proof that **government** will pilot **private** **marketplace** tools when **rail** **offtake** stalls without **road** **legs**. Open questions: **standard** **trade** **documents** on **blockchain** theatre versus **PDF** reality, and **regional** **payments** **rails** for **trucker** **pay**.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.* If you move **tons**, hire **operations** leaders who have cleaned a **customs** **yard** at **2 a.m.** **Global positioning system (GPS)** traces are not a **moat**; **reliability** **service-level agreements (SLAs)** are.
