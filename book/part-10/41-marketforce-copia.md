# 41. MarketForce and Copia: Informal Retail Digitization

## Lead

A kiosk owner transitions from verbal reorders to app-assisted procurement. Each order creates data: product velocity, spend patterns, repayment behavior, and inventory rhythm.

The rail is digitally mediated informal retail supply. Builders include B2B commerce teams, field sales networks, and merchant-support operations. Users include small retailers whose survival depends on reliable stock and price discipline. Referees include supplier terms, investor timelines, and macroeconomic pressure.

Behavior shifted as neighborhood retail gained access to structured ordering and broader supplier channels.

The shadow is unforgiving economics. Distribution cost, default risk, and thin retailer margins challenge scale narratives.

Bridge: with transaction data rising, credit products became easier to embed and harder to govern.
