# 41. MarketForce and Copia: Informal Retail Digitization

## Lead

Two **Nairobi** answers to the same problem: how do you serve the **~80%** of **sub-Saharan** household retail that still runs through informal shops—without pretending a **Western** **e-commerce** **checkout** maps cleanly onto **cash**, **trust**, and **last-mile** physics? **MarketForce**’s **RejaReja** super-app, described by **TechCrunch** in **2022**, lets traders source hundreds of **FMCG** **stock-keeping units (SKUs)** for next-day delivery, pay digitally, sell **utility** **airtime**, and access **buy now, pay later (BNPL)**-style inventory finance—**CEO** **Tesh Mbaabu** framed it as “merchant inventory financing… like an overdraft facility” after a pilot tripled order stock value ([TechCrunch — MarketForce USD 40M Series A][tc-mf-raise]).

**Copia Global** chose a different wedge: **business-to-consumer (B2C)** catalogue commerce for **middle-** and **low-income** buyers, using **~30,000** local **agents**—often existing shopkeepers—as trusted pickup points and **commission** earners. **TechCrunch** relayed **Copia**’s claim of **10 million+** orders, **24–48** hour delivery, and *no* customer delivery fee—offset by supplier aggregation and agent **density** ([TechCrunch — Copia USD 50M Series C][tc-copia]).

The shared **rail** is **informal retail** as a **data**-addressable **channel**—orders, repayment history, and stock velocity becoming inputs for **credit** and **brand** **analytics**. **Builders** are **MarketForce** co-founders **Mbaabu** and **Mesongo Sibuti** (**2018**) and **Copia** founders **Tracey Turner** and **Jonathan Lewis** (**2013**), plus armies of field reps. **Users** are kiosk owners and rural consumers. **Referees** are **supplier** **credit** committees, **DFI** investors (**U.S. International Development Finance Corporation (DFC)** appears in **Copia**’s cap table per **TechCrunch**), and the **macro** **venture** cycle.

The tension is **inclusion narrative versus margin math**. The same **TechCrunch** ecosystem that celebrated **USD 40 million** rounds documented, a year later, **MarketForce**’s **USD 8 million** equity never wiring because of “failed capital calls,” **250** layoffs, and routes cut from **700** to **400**—while **Mbaabu** argued the firm had turned **gross** profitable on trimmed scope ([TechCrunch — MarketForce belt][tc-mf-belt]).

## Context

Both companies stack atop [Part III](../part-03/index.md) payments culture and [Part VI](../part-06/index.md) merchant rails—**Cellulant** co-founder **Ken Njoroge** joined **MarketForce**’s board per **TechCrunch** (**2022**), a literal bridge between **payments** plumbing and **B2B** commerce ([TechCrunch — MarketForce USD 40M Series A][tc-mf-raise]).

## History

### MarketForce: from **SaaS** to **RejaReja**

**RejaReja** launched **2020** off **MarketForce**’s formal-retail **software** roots; the **February 2022** round (half debt, half equity) brought total funding to **USD 42.5 million** and targeted **250,000** merchants on aggressive timelines **Mbaabu** later conceded were overheated ([TechCrunch — MarketForce USD 40M Series A][tc-mf-raise]; [TechCrunch — MarketForce belt][tc-mf-belt]).

### Copia: agents as infrastructure

**January 2022**’s **USD 50 million** **Series C** (Goodwell-led) took lifetime funding to **USD 83.5 million** and emphasised **agent** shops as always-on pickup nodes—**CEO** **Tim Steel** argued the model dodged failed home drops common in pure **door-to-door** **e-commerce** ([TechCrunch — Copia USD 50M Series C][tc-copia]).

### 2023: synchronised belt-tightening

**Twiga**’s **August 2023** restructure article explicitly bracketed **Copia** and **MarketForce** among **B2B** platforms downsizing amid **venture** scarcity—use it as cross-case evidence, not adjudication ([TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-footnote]).

## Product and mechanics

### Composite scene: Two phones on the counter

*Composite:* A **kiosk** owner scrolls **RejaReja** for **soda** crates while a customer pays for a **Copia** voucher at the same counter—the **hardware** is one **SIM**, the **rails** are parallel **B2B** and **B2C** graphs.

**MarketForce**’s asset-light model leans on manufacturer and distributor partners for **warehouses** and **trucks**; **Copia** leans on **agents** and “proprietary **Copia** **Logistics**” language in corporate statements quoted by **TechCrunch** ([TechCrunch — MarketForce USD 40M Series A][tc-mf-raise]; [TechCrunch — Copia USD 50M Series C][tc-copia]).

## Business model and incentives

**MarketForce** monetises **distribution** spreads, **BNPL** **interest**, and **brand** **intelligence** dashboards; **Copia** monetises **product** **margin** and **agent** **commissions** (**TechCrunch** notes **>30%** agent income uplift claims) ([TechCrunch — MarketForce USD 40M Series A][tc-mf-raise]; [TechCrunch — Copia USD 50M Series C][tc-copia]).

## Regulation and referees

Embedded **credit** drags both firms toward **Central Bank of Kenya (CBK)**-adjacent questions when **lending** characteristics dominate; **Data Protection Act**, **2019** governs **merchant** and **shopper** **personally identifiable information (PII)** ([Data Protection Act][dpa]). **MarketForce**’s **2023** interview cited a **Kenya** government **distribution** programme—policy tailwind when real, execution risk when politics shift ([TechCrunch — MarketForce belt][tc-mf-belt]).

## Adoption in Kenya

**MarketForce** named **five** markets and **20** towns with hundreds of routes; **Copia** emphasised **rural** **Kenya**/**Uganda** **agent** grids and urban/diaspora send-home use cases ([TechCrunch — MarketForce belt][tc-mf-belt]; [TechCrunch — Copia USD 50M Series C][tc-copia]).

## Ecosystem effects

Together they trained **FMCG** multinationals to see kiosks as **data** endpoints—prelude to [Part 11](../part-11/index.md) **credit** products.

## Setbacks and controversies

Delayed **supplier** payments, **down** rounds, and **layoffs** belong in any sober account—anchor claims to **TechCrunch**’s **2023** reporting rather than forum gossip ([TechCrunch — MarketForce belt][tc-mf-belt]; [TechCrunch — Twiga restructure][tc-twiga-footnote]).

## Competition and alternatives

**Wasoko**/**Sokowatch**, **TradeDepot**, **Twiga**, traditional **distributors**, and **open-air** **markets**.

## Legacy and open questions

Legacy: **informal retail** became a **fundable** **category** with explicit **BNPL** and **agent-network** playbooks. Open questions: **consolidation**, **regulation** of **embedded** **lending**, and whether **government** **subsidy** programmes help or crowd out **private** **rails**.

## Builder read

*Interpretation.* **Capital** **called** beats **capital** **committed**. **Route**-level **contribution** margin is the truth serum—aggregate **gross merchandise value (GMV)** is theatre.
