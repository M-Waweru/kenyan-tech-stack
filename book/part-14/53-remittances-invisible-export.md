# 53. Remittances: Kenya's Invisible Export

## Lead

A transfer from London or Minneapolis can arrive as school fees, medicine, rent stability, or inventory capital by morning. Remittances are often discussed as macroeconomic statistics, but at household level they are timing tools that prevent crises from cascading.

The rail is cross-border household liquidity. Builders include remittance companies, bank corridors, mobile-money bridges, and payout networks. Users include recipient families and small businesses reliant on periodic support. Referees include foreign-exchange policy, AML compliance, and pricing oversight.

Behavior shifted as families integrated external income streams into everyday planning. This increased resilience during local shocks and supported investment in education and small enterprise.

The shadow includes transfer fees, exchange-rate opacity, and dependence on volatile external labor markets.

Bridge: digital assets emerged as alternative channels for value storage and movement.
