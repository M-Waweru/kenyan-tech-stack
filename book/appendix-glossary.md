# Appendix C: Glossary and Acronym Guide

Use this glossary as the canonical editorial reference for Kenya-specific terms, product names, and regulatory acronyms used across the book.

## Core Terms

| Term | Expansion / Meaning | Practical Context in This Book |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PayBill | M-PESA business collection number for institution or bill-style payments | Used by schools, landlords, churches, utilities, and SMEs for structured collections and reconciliation. |
| Till (Till Number) | Merchant acceptance number for point-of-sale style customer payments | Common on kiosk stickers, retail checkout counters, and small-business collections. |
| chama | Informal savings/investment group (community table-banking model) | Core community finance coordination unit, often evolving from notebook ledgers to digital group tooling. |
| SACCO | Savings and Credit Cooperative Organization | Regulated cooperative finance institutions that provide savings products and member lending. |
| USSD | Unstructured Supplementary Service Data | Session-based mobile menu channel for feature phones and low-bandwidth interactions. |
| Fuliza | Safaricom overdraft product embedded in M-PESA transactions | Illustrates ambient borrowing behavior and embedded short-term credit risk. |

## Regulatory and Institutional Acronyms

| Acronym | Expansion | Why It Matters in the Narrative |
| --- | --- | --- |
| DCP | Digital Credit Providers | Marks the licensing and conduct regime that formalized digital lending behavior. |
| ODPC | Office of the Data Protection Commissioner | Defines privacy and lawful data-use expectations for platforms and state systems. |
| CA | Communications Authority of Kenya | Telecom and cyber-sector regulator whose statistics and advisories frame market reality. |
| CBK | Central Bank of Kenya | Financial regulator shaping payment, banking, and digital-credit safeguards. |

## Editorial Lint Checklist (Acronym First-Use Enforcement)

Apply this checklist in every chapter before publication:

- [ ] Expand each acronym at first mention in that chapter (example: `USSD (Unstructured Supplementary Service Data)`).
- [ ] After first expansion, use only the acronym for consistency, unless readability requires repeating the full term.
- [ ] Expand product names that may be opaque to first-time readers at first mention (example: `Fuliza overdraft facility`).
- [ ] For Kenya-specific nouns (for example `chama`, `PayBill`, `Till`), include a short plain-language description at first chapter mention.
- [ ] Verify regulator references are fully expanded at first use in chapter (`CBK`, `CA`, `ODPC`).
- [ ] Confirm chapter has no unexplained all-caps token with two or more letters.
- [ ] If a chapter introduces a new acronym not listed here, add it to this appendix in the same commit.
