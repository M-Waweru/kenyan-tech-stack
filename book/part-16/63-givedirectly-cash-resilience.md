# 63. GiveDirectly: Cash as Climate and Shock Infrastructure

## Lead

When households experience drought, medical emergencies, or income collapse, unrestricted cash can restore agency faster than tightly scripted aid flows.

The rail is direct digital transfer for resilience. Builders include transfer program operators, payment partners, and verification systems. Users include vulnerable households deciding priorities based on local reality. Referees include evaluators, donors, and public accountability mechanisms.

Behavior shifted toward broader acceptance of cash-first intervention logic where evidence supports impact.

The shadow includes targeting disputes, social tension in mixed-beneficiary communities, and fraud prevention burdens.

Bridge: as survival rails matured, influence rails around creators and narratives reshaped public life.
